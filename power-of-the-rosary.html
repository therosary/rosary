Our Lady's Own Words

The 15 Promises of the Rosary

Our Lady is said to have revealed these fifteen promises to Saint Dominic and Blessed Alan de la Roche for all those who pray the Rosary devoutly. They have been handed down through Dominican tradition for centuries and have inspired generations of faithful Catholics.

Source & Tradition These promises were revealed by Our Lady to Saint Dominic de Guzmán (1170–1221), founder of the Dominican Order, and later confirmed through Blessed Alan de la Roche (1428–1475). They have been part of Dominican tradition and Catholic devotion for over 800 years.
1
Whoever shall faithfully serve me by the recitation of the Rosary, shall receive signal graces.
2
I promise my special protection and the greatest graces to all those who shall recite the Rosary.
3
The Rosary shall be a powerful armor against hell, it will destroy vice, decrease sin, and defeat heresies.
4
It will cause virtue and good works to flourish; it will obtain for souls the abundant mercy of God; it will withdraw the hearts of people from the love of the world and its vanities, and will lift them to the desire of eternal things.
5
The soul which recommends itself to me by the recitation of the Rosary shall not perish.
6
Whoever shall recite the Rosary devoutly, applying himself to the consideration of its Sacred Mysteries, shall never be conquered by misfortune. God will not chastise him in His justice, he shall not perish by an unprovided death; if he be just, he shall remain in the grace of God and become worthy of eternal life.
7
Whoever shall have a true devotion for the Rosary shall not die without the Sacraments of the Church.
8
Those who are faithful to recite the Rosary shall have, during their life and at their death, the light of God and the plenitude of His graces; at the moment of death they shall participate in the merits of the Saints in Paradise.
9
I shall deliver from purgatory those who have been devoted to the Rosary.
10
The faithful children of the Rosary shall merit a high degree of glory in Heaven.
11
You shall obtain all you ask of me by the recitation of the Rosary.
12
All those who propagate the Holy Rosary shall be aided by me in their necessities.
13
I have obtained from my Divine Son that all the advocates of the Rosary shall have for intercessors the entire Celestial Court during their life and at the hour of their death.
14
All who recite the Rosary are my children, and brothers and sisters of my only Son Jesus Christ.
15
Devotion to my Rosary is a great sign of predestination.

Why It Works

The Spiritual Power Explained

The Rosary is not magic, and it is not superstition. Its power flows from specific spiritual realities — rooted in Scripture, Tradition, and the nature of prayer itself.

  • 📖
    It is Gospel Meditation Every mystery is a scene from the Gospels. You spend 20 minutes meditating on the life of Jesus — his birth, ministry, passion, and resurrection. This is not empty repetition. It is deep encounter with Scripture.
  • 🤲
    It Engages Body, Mind and Soul Your hands hold the beads. Your lips pray the words. Your mind holds the mystery. Your heart opens to God. The Rosary integrates the whole person in prayer — unlike purely mental prayer, it anchors the wandering mind.
  • 👑
    It Invokes Mary's Intercession Mary is the most powerful intercessor in the Church. At Cana she interceded once and Christ performed his first miracle. She never stops interceding for those who call upon her. Each Hail Mary is a rose placed at her feet.
  • 🔁
    Repetition Creates Depth The repetition of the Hail Mary is not vain repetition — it is like the rhythm of breathing. It creates a contemplative state in which the heart can go deeper into the mystery than any single prayer could carry it.
  • 📅
    Daily Fidelity Transforms Lives The power of the Rosary is not felt in one prayer — it is cumulative. Those who pray it daily for months and years speak of a transformation so deep it is difficult to describe. Conversion, healing, peace, answered prayers.
